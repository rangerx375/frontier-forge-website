Section 4 — Authorization to Treat

I, the undersigned parent or legal guardian of the camper named in Section 1, authorize the Frontier Forge Institute ("FFI"), its staff, the camp's on-site Registered Nurse or medic, and any consulting physician, dentist, hospital, or emergency medical services personnel to provide such routine first aid, medical treatment, dental treatment, surgical treatment, hospital care, and transportation as may be deemed necessary for the health and well-being of the camper during the period of the camp.

FFI will make reasonable efforts to contact me before any non-emergency treatment is rendered, but in a true medical emergency, treatment may proceed without prior contact if delay would jeopardize the camper's health. I authorize the release of medical records and information necessary for treatment.

I understand and accept that I am financially responsible for any medical, dental, hospital, ambulance, or other healthcare expenses incurred on behalf of the camper, including any expenses not covered by my insurance. FFI is not responsible for these costs.

On-site medical authority. The camp will have a Registered Nurse or qualified medic on-site full-time during the program. The on-site medical authority will assess all injuries and illnesses, administer medications per Section 5, and determine when off-site medical evaluation is required.

Section 7 — Risk & Liability Waiver

THIS IS A LEGAL DOCUMENT — READ CAREFULLY BEFORE SIGNING.

Acknowledgment of inherent risks. I understand and acknowledge that participation in the Frontier Forge Institute program involves inherent risks of physical injury, illness, property damage, and in extreme cases, permanent disability or death. These risks include, but are not limited to: slips, trips, and falls on uneven terrain; cuts, abrasions, sprains, fractures, and other orthopedic injuries; injuries from tools, ropes, firearms, or other equipment; drowning or near-drowning; insect stings, tick bites, and exposure to vector-borne illness; allergic reactions; heat-related illness, hypothermia, or weather-related injury; and the negligent acts of other participants. I acknowledge that these risks cannot be eliminated regardless of the care taken to avoid injury.

Voluntary participation. I confirm that my son's participation in this program is entirely voluntary, that he is in suitable physical and mental condition to participate, and that I have informed him of the nature of these activities. I understand he may be removed from the program for safety, behavioral, or medical reasons at the sole discretion of FFI leadership.

Release and waiver. In consideration of my son's acceptance into the program, I, on behalf of myself, my son, and our heirs, executors, administrators, and assigns, hereby RELEASE, WAIVE, DISCHARGE, AND COVENANT NOT TO SUE Frontier Forge Institute, its directors, officers, employees, volunteers, cadre, instructors, agents, contractors (including but not limited to Appalachian Bible College and the Appalachian Bible College Outdoors team), property owners, lessors (including Alpine Ministries), and all other persons or entities acting on FFI's behalf (collectively, the "Released Parties"), from any and all liability, claims, demands, actions, and causes of action whatsoever arising out of or related to any loss, damage, illness, or injury, including death, that may be sustained by my son or by any property belonging to him while participating in the program.

Indemnification. I further agree to INDEMNIFY AND HOLD HARMLESS the Released Parties from any loss, liability, damage, or costs (including attorney's fees) they may incur due to my son's participation in the program, whether caused by the negligence of the Released Parties or otherwise, to the maximum extent permitted by West Virginia law.

Severability and governing law. If any provision of this packet is found unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain in full force. This packet is governed by the laws of the State of West Virginia.
